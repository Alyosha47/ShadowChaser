About

ShadowChaser is developed by followtheshadow, a loose consortium of friends who have been following eclipses together since May 10, 1994. But although there are many eclipse-adventure-planning tools out there, we still missed certain features. Search-by-location, for example, accurate local time zone resolution, historical weather planning, shadow-mapping from local topographies, and offline map caching for in-the-field use. We’ve tried to cover all the bases in one convenient package. If you find any bugs, please let us know — and don’t stare at the sun!

Eclipse circumstances are calculated using the Besselian element method as described in Jean Meeus, Astronomical Algorithms (Willmann-Bell, 2nd ed. 1998), Chapter 54, and the NASA Five Millennium Canon of Solar Eclipses (Espenak & Meeus). No lunar limb correction is applied.

Besselian elements: Espenak & Meeus, Five Millennium Canon of Solar Eclipses: −1999 to +3000, NASA/TP-2006-214141.
