About

ShadowChaser is developed by followtheshadow, a loose consortium of friends who have been following eclipses together since May 10, 1994. Although there are many eclipse-adventure-planning tools out there, we still missed certain features — search-by-location, accurate local timezone resolution, historical weather planning, shadow-mapping from local topographies, and offline map caching for in-the-field use. We’ve tried to cover all the bases in one convenient package. If you find any bugs, please let us know — and don’t stare at the sun!

Data Sources

Eclipse catalogue & Besselian elements — Espenak & Meeus, Five Millennium Canon of Solar Eclipses: −1999 to +3000, NASA/TP-2006-214141. Eclipse circumstances computed via the Besselian element method (Jean Meeus, Astronomical Algorithms, Willmann-Bell, 2nd ed. 1998, Ch. 54). No lunar limb correction applied.

Delta T (ΔT = TT − UT) — Best available source per date: 1973 to last month — USNO observed monthly tables (deltat.data); last month to ~5 years out — USNO quarterly forecast (deltat.preds); −720 to 2050 (outside the USNO range) — Espenak–Meeus piecewise polynomial; before −720 and after 2050 — Stephenson, Morrison & Hohenkerk (2016) LOD integral extrapolation.

Basemap — Online: OpenFreeMap Liberty vector style (openfreemap.org). Offline: Natural Earth public-domain geodata (naturalearthdata.com).

Timezone lookup — Offline polygon database via tz-lookup. Elevation auto-fill via Open-Elevation API when online.
